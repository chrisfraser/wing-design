# Covered: parasitic vs induced drag; aspect ratio as the endurance lever

Lesson 0003 delivered. Decomposed the Lesson-1 parabolic polar into parasitic (C_D0, lift-independent
floor) + induced (C_L²/(π·AR·e), the price of lift on a finite wing). Established the strategic result:
at the endurance point induced drag = 3× parasitic (~75% of total), so **raising aspect ratio is the
highest-leverage move for endurance** — with the honest caveats (structural weight; narrower chord ⇒
lower Reynolds number).

This pulls aspect ratio / induced drag forward from the originally-planned Lesson 8 into the foundations,
because it's the natural payoff of the drag split and the strongest motivator for the wing's overall
shape. The endurance "twist" (C_L^1.5/C_D) was already covered in Lesson 1, so the separate planned
"design-C_L" lesson is folded in here.

Implication: the two open threads are now (a) profile/parasitic drag ⇒ airfoil + **Reynolds number**
(next, Lesson 4 — and the defining feature of this drone's regime), and (b) induced drag ⇒ full
planform/wing-loading treatment (later, before XFLR5). No learner-demonstrated evidence yet this session.
